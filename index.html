Monolith vs. Microservices

Scale & Complexity

The Executive Trade-off

The choice is economic, not just technical. Monoliths optimize for initial velocity but create a "complexity ceiling." Microservices optimize for infinite scale but impose a high upfront "DevOps Tax."

Re-architect when:

  • Deployment frequency drops (e.g., from daily to bi-weekly).
  • Scaling one feature (Search) requires scaling the entire monolith.
  • Team size > 50 (Conway's Law indicates communication breakdown).
  • Monolith: Low CapEx. High long-term OpEx (maintenance/tech debt).
  • Microservices: High CapEx (Kubernetes, CI/CD). Lower long-term OpEx *if* automated correctly.
Cost Curve Projection
Monolith

Unified codebase. In-process function calls (nanoseconds). Single database ACID transactions.

User -> App (All Logic) -> DB
Microservices

Distributed services. Network calls (latency). Polyglot persistence. API Gateway required.

User -> Gateway -> [Auth] + [Cart]

Deep Dive: Trade-offs

Monoliths utilize ACID transactions (Easy, consistent). Microservices require "Eventual Consistency" (BASE) using patterns like Sagas or Distributed Locks (Hard).
PM Scenario

"We're building 'Tinder for Dogs'. Monolith or Microservices?"

The Trap: Jumping to "Microservices" because it sounds scalable.

The Best Answer: "I'd advocate for a **well-structured Monolith**. Our risk right now isn't scale; it's product-market fit. A monolith allows us to iterate fastest with a small team. We will design clear module boundaries (Modulith) so we can extract services later *if* we succeed."

C-Suite Narrative

"Microservices are not an engineering project; they are an organizational investment. We are paying a higher upfront 'tax' in complexity to buy the option of infinite team scalability later. If we don't plan to hire 100+ engineers, we don't need to pay this tax."

SaaS Economics

The Rule of 40

The golden rule for mature SaaS: Growth Rate % + Profit Margin % should be > 40%.

It balances the "Growth at all costs" mindset with profitability.

Company A: 10% Growth + 10% Margin = 20%
Company B: 30% Growth + 15% Margin = 45%

Why RAG?

Solves Hallucinations and Data Privacy. By grounding the AI in your specific documents (Vector DB) before it answers, you get accurate, citable responses without training the model on your data.
